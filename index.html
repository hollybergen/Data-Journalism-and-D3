Correlations Discovered Between Obesity and Poverty

We pulled census data from across the United States in 2014, comparing variables poverty and obesity. You can see from the chart there looks to be a trend of positive correlation between these variables, meaning a correlation of higher poverty to higher obesity. There are of course some outliers, such as Washington, D.C. and Colorado which show high poverty to low obesity, or Maryland and New Hampshire which show higher obesity to low poverty. The states of Mississippi, Louisiana, Alabama, Arkansas, and West Virginia all have high obesity as well as high poverty.

These results are interesting, considering that in non-industrialized nations, we would expect poorness and poverty to be correlated with malnutrition and hunger. However these results seem to suggest that in an industrialized nation such as the U.S., healthier diets come at a higher cost, and there are many cheap, available options such as fast food.
